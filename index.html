About

I have always dreamed of telling meaningful stories. As a child, I imagined fairies and woodland spirits watching over me, and that imagination soon turned into writing novels at 9. Later, I created short Dungeons & Dragons sessions—whether spooky, mysterious, or just plain silly—and discovered a love for guiding players through the chaos I crafted. This passion naturally led me to narrative-driven video games, from playful, pun-filled tales to deeper, branching stories. I also had a blast designing platformer levels, and I can't wait to merge both worlds by creating a narrative-driven level someday.

Eternal Wounds

Being a Puzzle Designer truly twists your mind.
Eternal Wounds is our final Bachelor’s project at Gamagora, created by a team of 17 made in Unreal. Balancing difficulty with player enjoyment was both the biggest challenge and the most rewarding part of working as a Level Designer. Through trial and error, we shaped complex but unique mechanics that, while fun, were initially hard for players to internalize. As Level Designers, we focused on guiding players clearly—while still giving them moments to pause, reflect, and feel the satisfaction of solving a puzzle.

Chasing the Light

Chasing the Light is a small Gamagora Game Jam project with a team of 4 on board. The theme was "If you don't see it, it doesn't exist." We created a platformer where light was crucial: players could safely move in lit areas, but risked their lives in the dark. Some platforms would block the way when illuminated. Designing a path to teach the light mechanics while challenging the player's perspective was tough. It required guiding them step by step and knowing when to let go. This game was certainly my first step towards becoming a Level Designer.

Bubble Market

"My house was made by SandCastle Constructions. Did an amazing job too, up until it caved in."
Bubble Market is a game made by 7 people for the Global Game Jam 2025 with the theme "Bubbles". After some discussion, we went for a speculative bubble game in which you play a trader dealing in bubbles before it pops. With various very expressive, diverse and colorful characters, our goal was to make as much puns as we could. Sea, the game caused quite a splash, definitely worth the dive.

Once Upon a Page

"If we’re not using our powers to blow stuff up, what's even the point?" - Emma, 11 yo
"Once Upon a Page" was a narrative-driven project where I created the characters, scenario, world-building, and dialogues. The tone was lighthearted, with a dad narrating stories where his children could shape the plot. I had fun writing storylines full of puns, with a cow (instead of humans) cowboy world and a villain called "Moot-Law." I also created flowcharts to track dialogue choices leading to different story outcomes.

  • Narrative Design
Styx

Styx is a narrative game where you play as an amnesiac sea captain sailing the River Styx. By guiding lost souls, he slowly regains his memories—eventually recalling his long-lost wife, now a ghost among many. Reunited, they can finally leave the underworld. I wrote the story and characters, and contributed to gameplay. The tone is melancholic and slow-paced. It is a project that I hold close to my heart and would love to develop further.

Adventure Ball

Adventure Ball is the result of a game made for an assignment in Unity. It is a platformer in which the player is transported to 3 different levels: desertic, volcanic and artic. It is overall a small game that made me realize the complexity and the fun of Level Design.
